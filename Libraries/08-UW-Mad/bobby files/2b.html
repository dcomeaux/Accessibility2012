In Depth

[View of WisCEL]

A post titled "A Friendly Wager on Votes for Women" from "Found in the Archives" relates the amusing story of two UW students who, in 1896, found a letter in a room in Ladies Hall (now Chadbourne Hall) that included a wager from twenty years prior. The wager, written by two previous inhabitants of the residence hall room, was on whether women would have obtained the right to vote by 1896. Read the full story here on "Found in the Archives," the Tumblr for the University Archives.
