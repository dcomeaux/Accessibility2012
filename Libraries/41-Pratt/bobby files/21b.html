About the Visual Resources Center (VRC)

 

The Visual Resources Center (VRC) is a division of the Pratt Institute Libraries, located on the 3rd floor of the Brooklyn Campus Library. The VRC houses a collection of over 120,000 architecture, art and design color slides; a growing digital image collection; scanning workstations for public use, and a copy stand that is available by appointment only.

The VRC is open to Pratt faculty, staff and students with a valid Pratt ID card, as well as to Pratt alumni with a valid Alumni Card and to visiting faculty during the summer (pre-college program) and winter sessions, though not all resources may be available to alumni. For more information on access to Pratt Libraries for alumni, see Alumni, Friends & Others. Contact the VRC by phone at (718) 636-3716, or by email at vrc@pratt.edu.

Digital Image Collections

 

Pratt Institute Libraries Digital Image Collection

The VRC maintains and oversees a collection of over 35,000 locally created and licensed images from the Visual Resources Center for use by Pratt faculty and students for research and teaching. These images are delivered together with the over 1.3 million images in the ARTstor Digital Library. For more information, visit the Images page.  

Archives & Special Collections Images on Flickr

The VRC also manages over 1,600 images from the Pratt Institute Libraries’ Archives and Special Collections, which are accessible to the general public on Flickr. ARTstor. For more information, visit the Images page.

35mm Slide Collection

 

35mm Slides

The slide collection is available to Pratt faculty, staff and students with a valid ID card who have registered in the library. Alumni with a valid Alumni Card and visiting faculty during the summer (Pre-college program) or winter sessions are ineligible to borrow slides. Slides are not to be used outside of Pratt Institute, and may not be duplicated.

Borrowing Slides

Slides must be checked out at the Visual Resources Center. All patrons must fill out a check-out form with their name, date, address, telephone number and department. Patrons must write under the corresponding medium the artist's name and the number of slides to be checked out. Faculty may borrow an unlimited number of slides for up to one month, but are encouraged to return slides before that time. Students may borrow up to 70 slides for up to one week.

Returning Slides

Patrons must return slides to the Visual Resources Center in-person during regular operating hours. Faculty must return slides within one month, grouped according to the check-out form. No partial returns will be accepted. It is very important that patrons make sure that all slides are checked in and that their account is cleared, as materials not cleared from a patron's account will be considered lost. If your account is not cleared, you will receive a bill for the lost or damaged slides.

Renewing Slides

Faculty may renew slides they have borrowed for an additional two weeks. Students may renew slides for an additional week. When renewing slides, all patrons must renew the entire set (box) of slides they have borrowed. Partial return of slides and partial renewals are not accepted. Additionally, slides may only be renewed once.

Reserving Slides

Patrons may reserve slides for one week only. Slides not picked up within one week are re-filed.

Repair and Replacement Charges

Charges for students, faculty and staff are $5.00 per broken glass, and $10.00 per lost slide or damaged film. Faculty with outstanding slides from previous semesters may not borrow slides for the current semester until all slides are returned or a lost charge of $10.00 per slides is paid to the library. Fines for students are $0.50 per day for overdue slide boxes.

Requesting Digital Images

 

Pratt Institute Libraries Digital Image Collection

Faculty with a valid Pratt ID card may request digital images for inclusion in the Pratt Institute Libraries Digital Image Collection, hosted online through ARTstor. Whenever possible, the VRC will purchase images from our vendors or will locate comparable images in the ARTstor Digital Library. When images are not readily available for purchase or through our ARTstor subscription, the VRC will produce digital images in-house via slide scanning, flatbed scanning, or digital photography.

To place an order faculty must fill out a Digital Image Request Form for each source from which they are requesting digital images. The form may be downloaded here, but must be delivered in person to the VRC during regular operating hours.

Image orders should be requested from the best quality reproductions available and will be processed on a first-come, first-served basis. Depending upon the length of the queue, orders of fewer than 100 images can typically be fulfilled in 2-3 weeks. Orders exceeding 100 images and broader collection development requests should be scheduled in consultation with the Visual Resources Curator.

Image orders will be delivered directly through the ARTstor Digital Library, and an email will be sent when they are ready for pick up.

Archives and Special Collections Images

The Pratt Institute Libraries may, under certain conditions, provide digital reproductions for materials held by its Archives, Special Collections, and Visual Resources departments. If reproductions are warranted, fees will be assessed according to the Reproduction Fee Schedule (PDF). For uses other than private study, scholarship, or research, use fees may also apply, and will be assessed according to the Use Fee Schedule (PDF).

Digital files are provided in JPEG format unless otherwise specified (e.g., TIFF, PDF). For printed materials, originals will be scanned at 300dpi. For slides, negatives, transparencies, photographic prints, and graphic art, the scan resolution will be increased to achieve a minimum of 3,000 pixels on the long dimension. Low-resolution files may be provided at a discounted rate, at the discretion of the Archivist or Visual Resources Curator.

Turnaround time is approximately 1 week. Orders needed in fewer than 3 days are considered Rush orders.

The Pratt Institute Libraries do not currently provide photographic film or print reproductions.

  • To request reproductions from the Pratt Institute Archives, contact Paul Schlotthauer, Archivist, at (718) 636-3686 or pschlott@pratt.edu.

  • To request reproductions for all other materials held by the Pratt Institute Libraries,contact Johanna Bauman, Visual Resources Curator, at (718) 636-3716 or jbauma91@pratt.edu.

Creating Digital Images

 

Flatbed Scanner

There is an Epson Expression 10000XL scanner available in the VRC on a first-come, first-served basis during the VRC’s regular operating hours. The scanning station is equipped with Adobe Photoshop and is connected to the Internet. Film and slide adapters are available for use with the scanner. Patrons should bring their own CD, DVD, or Flash drive to transfer scanned images from the scanning workstation. Flash drives are also available for checkout from the VRC and Circulation Desk (1st floor).

35mm Slide Scanners

A Nikon Super CoolScan 5000 ED slide scanner is also available during the VRC’s regular operating hours . Appointments are strongly encouraged, and should be made at least 24 hours in advance by contacting the VRC at 718-636-3716 or vrc@pratt.edu. Patrons should bring their own CD, DVD, or Flash drive to transfer scanned images from the scanning workstation. Flash drives are also available for checkout from the VRC and Circulation Desk (1st floor).

Copy Stand

Due to renovations in the VRC, the copy stand will be unavailable for use from Monday, January 26th, 2012 through February 3rd, 2012. For more information, please call the VRC at 718-636-3716 or email vrc@pratt.edu. There is a copy stand outfitted with Tungsten lights that is available to Pratt faculty, staff and students with a validated ID card. Alumni with a validated Alumni Card and visiting faculty during the summer (Pre-college program) or winter sessions may also use the copy stand. The VRC can provide a 35mm film or digital camera, though patrons are encouraged to bring their own equipment. The copy stand measures approximately 18 by 24 inches. The copy stand is available by appointment only from 9AM to 5PM. Please call the VRC at 718-636-3716 to make an appointment, or email vrc@pratt.edu.
