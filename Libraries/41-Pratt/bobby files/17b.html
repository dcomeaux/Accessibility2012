Borrowing Items from the Pratt Institute Libraries

The Library circulation policies support the Libraries' mission by insuring accountability of library materials and insuring their availability to members of the Institute community and other library users on an equitable basis.

Service to users is the first priority of the Library.

  • Pratt faculty, students and staff members are permitted to borrow Library materials given they have a current, validated Pratt ID card.
  • Pratt faculty, students and staff members are permitted to borrow Library materials given they have submitted a signed Library Registration Form to the Library Circulation desk.
  • To check out Library materials, patrons must present their valid Pratt ID card to the circulation desk clerk at the time of check out.
  • When borrowing Library materials patrons agree to abide by the assigned due dates and to return the materials in good order.

Failure to meet these conditions may result in fines and/or the loss of borrowing privileges.  

***Patrons CANNOT check out items using someone else's ID.***

How Many Items May be Borrowed?

  • Undergraduate students:  A total of 10 items from the Library.
  • Graduate students: A total of 20 items from the Library.
  • Staff members: A total of 20 items from the Library.
  • Faculty members: A total of 20 items from the Library.

Circulating 4-week loan books may be renewed online via PrattCat.

Multi-media items and slides must be borrowed from and returned to Multi-Media Services and the Visual Resources Center respectively.

Interlibrary Loan (ILL)

  • ONLY Pratt faculty, students and staff in good Library standing may use ILL.
  • ONLY Pratt faculty, students and staff with a valid Pratt ID, with a current validation sticker may use ILL.
  • Alumni, Friends of the Library and Visiting Scholars CANNOT use ILL.
  • Anyone with a pattern of overdue/lost ILL items may not use ILL services.

For more detailed information on Pratt ILL services go to the Libraries’ ILL page.

[top]

 

Fines

 

Paying Late Fines

  • All fines must be paid in the Bursar's Office; the Library cannot accept payments.
  • Library fines can be paid by check, credit card, exact change or money order.
  • The Bursar does not give change and will apply any balances owed to the patron's tuition.
  • In order to pay fines, you must obtain a fine slip at the Circulation Desk which indicates the amount owed, and submit the slip with payment at the Bursar's Office.
  • The Bursar's Office will provide you with a yellow receipt that must be returned to the Circulation Desk in order to clear your fines.

 

Outstanding Fines

Students with outstanding fines amounting to or exceeding $10.00 cannot borrow books, including reserve materials, or use ILL until fines are paid.

Students with outstanding fines of any amount at the end of their final semester cannot graduate until fines are paid.

 

Lost Item Fines

All Library patrons (faculty, staff and students) are required to pay for lost items on their Library record.

  • The fine for a lost book is $90.00 (default)
    • $75.00 price for the book replacement
    • $15.00 for processing the book
  • An additional $15.00 is charged when a softcover is replacing a hardcover book

Replacement fees for picture files are $25.00.

 

Students with Incompletes

Students with incompletes not resolved within 30 days after the end of the term in which the incomplete is recorded, who are not currently registered for classes, may apply to their department chairs for letters of endorsement.

In Brooklyn contact the Circulation Desk at 718-636-3420, and in Manhattan, Jean Hines (212-647-7547) may provide an extension for Library access.

Renew

  • Regular circulating 4-week loan books may be renewed online via PrattCat.
    **see Borrowing tables above for restrictions on other items.**
  • Patrons with overdue items and/or fines will not be able to renew items online.
  • Patrons may also renew items in person at the Library Circulation desks or by phone.
  • In order to renew items by phone, patrons must speak to a Library staff person; voice mail renewals are not allowed.
  • To renew Brooklyn items via phone call 718-636-3420, ext. 1.
  • To renew Manhattan items via phone call 212-647-7546.
  • Please be aware that renewal dates are based on the day the item is renewed, not the item’s initial due date.


[top]

 

Book Return Drop Box (Brooklyn Campus)

  • The book drop located outside the Library is for patrons to use to return books when the Library is closed.
  • The book drop located inside the Library is for patrons to use to return regular, circulating books.
  • Neither drop boxes are to be used to return multi-media items or slides. Returning multi-media items and/or slides in the outside or inside book drops can damage the items.
  • Patrons will be charged fees for damaging Library items.
  • All multi-media items and 35mm slides must be returned direcly to the respective circulation desks/Centers during their regular hours.
  • Reserve materials should not be returned through the Circulation Desk drop box
  • Reserve materials should be handed directly to a circulation clerk, as they risk accruing late fines.


[top]

 

Non-Circulating Books

Books marked "non-circulating" or "reference" cannot be checked out of the Library without special permission.

  • Current faculty, students and staff in good standing with the Library may check out, upon a Librarian's approval, non-circulating stacks books. For approval go to the Reference Desk to ask a librarian.
  • Librarians may decide not to circulate the material if the material is deemed too fragile or rare.
  • Librarians may decide not to circulate the material if the faculty member has current blocks in the Libraries' system. (A block is defined as overdue material that the system considers "Lost.")
  • All material should be returned to the Circulation Desk.


[top]

 

Summer Privileges

  • All registered students (with summer or upcoming fall stickers) and staff may check out books during the summer term.
  • Adjunct and full-time faculty with continuing appointments may continue to borrow materials over the summer.
  • Visiting faculty with no confirmed appointment for the fall semester are limited to reading privileges during the summer.
  • Students with summer residence hall contracts or summer work awards, who are not registered for the summer term, are eligible for summer validation of Pratt ID cards with proof of residency or work (letter from supervisor).
  • They may have access to the Library, with reading and borrowing privileges.
  • Students who have pre-registered and prepaid for the fall semester also have summer privileges with proof of tuition pre-payment.
  • Pre-College Program students may be allowed access to the stacks, and to use the Library for research.
  • In Brooklyn contact the Circulation Desk at 718-636-3420. For Manhattan, consult Jean Hines (212) 647-7547.
