Classroom, Viewing, and Presentation Rooms in Hodges Library

Integrated User Services

[film] Viewing Rooms                                                                       [Need help?]Need help?

Library viewing rooms provide classroom space for those using media or other library resources as part of a class session. UT Faculty, Lecturers, or Graduate Teaching Assistants may reserve viewing rooms. Reservations on behalf of student groups must be made by the group's faculty advisor.

[laptop] Library Instruction Rooms

Library instruction rooms are set aside for Library instruction and orientation sessions. Only UT Libraries faculty and staff may may reserve instruction rooms.

Instruction room schedule

[presentation] Presentation Rooms

The Practice Presentation Room (236, Commons South) provides UTK students, faculty, and staff a place to practice presentations, hold meetings, or conduct videoconferences. When not reserved for training or practice, this room is available on a first-come-first-served basis.

The Martha L. King Room (220C, Commons North) is a small presentation room with a wall-mounted interactive LCD panel and a DVD-VCR combo unit.
