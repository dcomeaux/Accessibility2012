Services for Visitors & Alumni

Welcome to the University Libraries, a central foundation for Tennessee's oldest land-grant and primary research university. We are here to assist you with any information need or research question that you may have.

The information below pertains only to Hodges Library. Please visit our Branch Library page for specific information on their services for visitors and alumni.

 

John C. Hodges Library

Building Access | Print Resources | Online Resources | Borrowing | Photocopies & Printing | Assistance | Groups | Directions & Parking | Computer Use

Building Access
Patrons are not required to sign-in or show identification in order to enter and use the Libraries.

 

Use of Print Resources
The Libraries' print collections are available to all users for in-house use. Visitors requiring materials from Library Storage, should use a Storage Request Form to order materials in advance.

 

Use of Online Collections and Resources
Visitors and alumni may use guest computers to access the Internet and the Libraries' electronic resources.

 

Borrowing Privileges
Visit the Circulation pages for information on borrowing privileges. Non-UT affiliates may also borrow materials by using Interlibrary Loan at their local public library or may purchase a borrower's card.

 

Photocopies and Printing
Making photocopies costs six cents per page and requires a copy card. Money may be added to a card at a copy card dispenser using cash bills. Printing from guest computers is not available. Alternatives to printing may be to save the information on a flash drive or e-mail the information to yourself.

 

Assistance
For assistance locating materials or using resources, visit the Research Services Desk in the Commons. If you have questions that you would like to ask prior to your visit, you may email your question or call.

 

Groups
Organized groups wanting to schedule a library tour cann fill out the reservation form or contact Library Outreach and Community Learning Services.

 

Directions and Parking
Visitors to Hodges Library should park at the University Center Parking Garage or at a street meter. Location and parking information is also available for the Agriculture & Veterinary Medicine Library.
See also: Campus Maps, Building Locator, and UT Parking Regulations.
Note: Parking is very difficult on home football game days.

 

Computer Use in Hodges Library
Visitors may use guest computers located on the ground floor to access the University Libraries’ collections and government publications.
Children must be supervised by an adult chaperone.
