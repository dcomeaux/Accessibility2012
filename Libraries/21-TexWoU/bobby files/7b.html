Circulation Fines & Fees Policies

This page defines the privileges and responsibilities of persons who borrow materials from the Libraries at Texas Woman’s University. These are continually reviewed and revised by appropriate TWU administrators to reflect current library operations.

If library materials are not returned within 30 days of the due date, or if fines exceed $5.00 *as of Sept. 1, 2010, borrowing privileges will be revoked and records at the Registrar's Office will be blocked.

  • Payment of Fines and Fees
    Payment of fines by cash (exact change only) or check are accepted at the Main Library, and Dallas Center Circulation Desks. Credit card payments by Visa, Mastercard, Discover, and American Express are only accepted at the Main Library Circulation Desk. Failure to pay fines and fees may result in your account being turned over to a collection agency.

  • A $30 fee will be charged for ALL RETURNED CHECKS including electronic/web checks. TWU processes all eligible returned checks through the Denton County District Attorney’s Hot Check Division. There will be an additional charge assessed by the Denton DA.
  • Notification of Fines and Fees
    Library patrons are responsible for returning all borrowed items on time, whether or not an overdue notice is received. A courtesy notice is sent 7 days in advance of the due date, as a reminder.
  • Overdue Notices are provided to patrons as a courtesy.
  • When an item becomes overdue, five (5) notices are sent to the patron:

    1. At 1 day overdue
    2. At 8 days overdue
    3. At 15 days overdue
    4. At 22 days overdue
    5. At 30 days overdue, at which point it is considered Lost and additional fees are applied

All notices are processed daily and are sent via the patron's TWU e-mail account by default.  Courtesy Card patrons will be notified using the email address on file. If there is no e-mail address, notices will be sent by U.S. postal mail to a patron's local address or if Faculty/Staff, by campus mail.  Failure to receive a notice does not excuse patrons from fines and fees. Borrowers are responsible for providing a current mailing address, email address, and telephone number in order to receive timely information from the TWU Libraries. Failure to provide this information can result in a revocation of all library privileges.

  • Fines For Overdue Materials
    Regular Circulating Materials:
    $.35 day per item to a maximum of $11.00 per item

    Reserve Materials:
    Hourly items: $3.00/hour-per item (accrues at 5 cents/minute)
    One day items: $5.00/day per item
    Weekly items: $5.00/day per item
    Laptops: $1.00/minute

All reserve materials have a maximum fine of $50.00 per item, except laptops ($25)

Interlibrary Loan (ILL) Materials:
$1.00/day per item to a maximum of $50.00 per item
Note: Due dates for ILL materials are determined by the lending institution. ILL materials cannot be renewed.
A $5.00 fee will be assessed for every ILL due date band that is removed from ILL materials.

Media Items:
Videocassettes, DVD's, and Media Kits: $1.00/day (maximum $31.00 fine)
Audiocassettes, CD's, and LP's: $.35/day (maximum $11.00 fine)
Only one (1) renewal is allowed on media materials and new due dates must be confirmed.
Please refer to Media Collection for more information on Media items.

Laptops:
$1.00/minute with a maximum of $25 fine

Headphones:
$3.00/hour

 Recalled Items:
When an item is recalled, a new due date is assigned to that item. For every day that it is overdue beyond the new date, a $2.00 fine will be assessed.
Interlibrary Loan items can be recalled by the lending institution at any time and are subject to additional fines.
