Trial Databases
  • Academic Search Complete ----Interactive Tutorial

    Popular and scholarly resources including journals, magazine, and newspaper articles.

  • Dissertations & Theses Full Text (ProQuest)

    All doctoral dissertations and some master's theses from most North American and many European colleges and universities. See also our Guide for locating dissertations & theses.

  • Google Scholar

    Enables specific searches of scholarly literature, including peer-reviewed papers, theses, books, pre-prints, abstracts, and technical reports.

  • JSTOR ----Interactive Tutorial

    Enables specific searches of scholarly literature, including peer-reviewed papers, theses, books, pre-prints, abstracts, and technical reports.

  • LexisNexis Academic ----Interactive Tutorial

    Newspapers and news magazines including general topic news, as well as company, industry, market, government and political news.

  • PsycINFO

    Articles, dissertations, and book chapters from all areas of psychology.

  • PubMed (National Library of Medicine)

    More than 20 million citations for biomedical literature from MEDLINE, life science journals, and online books. Citations may include links to full-text content from PubMed Central and publisher web sites.

  • RefWorks

    Create your own personal database, import references from your institution's databases, automatically format your papers and bibliographies in seconds.
    For more information: RefWorks Guide.

  • Web of Science

    Includes the Citation Indexes for science, social sciences and arts & humanities, and indexes core journals in these areas. Premier database for cited reference searching.

  • WorldCat (FirstSearch)

    A catalog of records for all type of material cataloged by libraries throughout the world. (Note: This version of WorldCat differs from WorldCat Local and WorldCat.org in that it displays full bibliographic information, but does not include article citations.)
