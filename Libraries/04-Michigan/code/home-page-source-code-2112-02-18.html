Spotlight 1

Arab Spring

Interested in knowing more about the “Arab Spring” (protest movement in the Middle East and North Africa)? Check out some scholarly online articles from the ArticlesPlus search engine and see the Near Eastern Studies research guide for recommended Arab news sources.

Spotlight 2

De Humani Corporis Fabrica Libri Septem by Andreas Vesalius

Originally published in 1543, De Humani Corporis Fabrica Libri Septem (On the fabric of the human body in seven books) is the world’s most famous work on anatomy.  The MLibrary's copy is held in the collections of the Taubman Health Sciences Library.

Spotlight 3

Historical Newspapers

Do you want to find out what happened in the news last year, 25 years ago, 100 years ago? MLibrary has full-text, online historical newspapers including major U.S. newspapers, and early American newspapers, as well as English-language newspapers from around the world.

Check out the News Sources Research Guide for information on our vast newspaper collection.
