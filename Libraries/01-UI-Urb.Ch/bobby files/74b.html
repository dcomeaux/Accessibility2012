Learning Commons

[Connections Spirograph] About the Learning Commons

The Learning Commons at the Undergraduate Library aims to extend the successful programs and services of the Undergraduate Library to meet the evolving learning, studying, research, and content creation needs of the 21^st Century undergraduate student. The Learning Commons at the Undergraduate Library isn't separate from the Undergraduate Library, but an extension of its core values, and representation of its philosophical approach to library services & programs

For the past 40 years, the Undergraduate Library has been the central hub of undergraduate research and learning on the University of Illinois Urbana campus. The LC@UGL is built on the principle that today's students need the library to not only be place where they can access the information resources they need to do their coursework, but a site that supports their entire research process, from engaging their curiosity through producing a final intellectual product-in whatever form they need that final project to take.

How do we do this?

Space

The LC @ UGL wants students to be comfortable. We are continuously working to develop & improve our spaces to match the ways students use them. Our goal is to provide flexible spaces that accommodate group-work & single study, with spaces for those who need quiet isolation and spaces where groups can work together.

We have silent study rooms (downstairs) and our Collaboratories provide space and tools for groups working together on projects and presentations.

We also have specialized areas for working with our Partners, for media editing, for comfortable browsing of new materials, and for watching films.

We try hard to be responsive to what you want. Is there something you'd like to see us do with our space? Let us know!

Equipment & Technology 

Computing: The LC@UGL offers a variety of technology, as flexibly as possible. We have dozens of PCs, and 8 Macs loaded for media editing. We have 18 laptops available for in-library use as well.

We recently began offering public gaming, with Wii, XBox & PlayStation consoles & controllers available for use in the UGL (you can check out games from UGL too!)

We circulate a lot of small technology as well -- MP3 players, GPS, TI-89 graphing calculators, USB thumb drives, digital cameras & video cameras, and so much more. Check out what you can check out!

We also partner with CITES Security Outreach to provide several Laptop Clinics each semester, where you can bring your equipment in for a check-up

Is there something you'd like us to circulate that don't have? Let us know!

Media 

The Undergraduate Library hosts the media collection for campus (there's great stuff over at the Music & performing Arts Library as well!), so for all your media needs, we're here. If you're looking to explore avant garde movements, the best of Bollywood, study award-winning cinematography, or just grab something to watch, we have it. Our media viewing booths hold the equipment you need to watch - laserdisc players, VCRs, and DVDs all hooked up to televisions for your viewing pleasure (we also sell earbuds at the Circulation Desk so you can watch without disturbing others)

We also have a significant game collection, including games, consoles and controllers for XBox, XBox 360, PlayStation 2 and 3, Wii, and others.

Want a game we don't have? Let us know!

Digital Display 

The Undergraduate Library highlights a digital image collection on campus and displays it on a flat screen television located in the Browsing/New Materials section. This digital display is updated periodically and showcases different pictures or artwork from the UIUC community, so be sure to check it out!

People 

The people make the place -- and we have great people. We work hard to be helpful, to be friendly, and to be knowledgeable. The 70+ people who work in the Learning Commons, and the Undergraduate Library care about making sure you can find the information or equipment you need. We're here to help!

You can email us, IM us, call us, drop us a suggestion, or stop by the Research or Circulation Desks for assistance - that's why we're here! We're also on Twitter and Facebook.

In addition to the staff of the Undergraduate Library, the Learning Commons is about making connections. We have campus partners for services & programs designed to meet the needs of undergraduate students. Some of our partners hold office hours at the Connections Desk (Study Abroad, General Advising, Resume Critiques from The Career Center), and some are spread throughout the Learning Commons & Undergraduate Library.

Our Campus Partners include:

  • The Writer's Workshop
  • McKinley Health Services
  • CITES
  • Division of General Studies Academic Advising
  • The Career Center
  • Office of Study Abroad
  • Illini Union Bookstore
  • Unit 1/Allen Hall
  • Residence Hall Library System
  • Division of Intercollegiate Athletics
  • The University Library

The LC@UGL is funded through a collaboration by the Division of Intercollegiate Athletics (DIA), University Library, and CITES.
