Featured Scholar

Each month during the academic year, the UWM Libraries highlight a UWM scholar who has recently worked closely with the Libraries. This month we are featuring:

[Marcus Fililppello]Marcus Filippello Department of History

Ph.D., University of California-Davis, 2010
M.B.A., University of Missouri, 1997

Marcus Filippello, Assistant Professor in UWM's History Department, will give a presentation, "Reading Landscapes and Hearing Voices in Benin," in the American Geographical Society Library's lecture series, UWM's Academic Adventurers, on March 2, 2012.

Professor Filippello's research interests include Sub-Saharan African history (especially Benin), environmental history, and history of development.

His dissertation, completed in 2010, "The Slow Road to Ketu: Colonial Resistance, Environmental Change and Post-Independence Autonomy in a Beninese Forest Community," is a social and environmental history of a road connecting Pobé and Ketu in southeastern Benin, West Africa.

Professor Filipello presented "Cementing National Identities in Dahomey and Benin: Understanding Development" at the 2011 African Conference, March 25-27, 2011 at the University of Texas at Austin.

In April, he will deliver a lecture, "'Black Earth’: Roads, Environmental Change, and Identity in a West African Forest Community," in the Conable Distinguished Lecture Series in International Studies at Rochester Institute of Technology, Rochester, NY.
