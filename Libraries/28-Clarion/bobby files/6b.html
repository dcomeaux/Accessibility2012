Virtual Campus Library Services

Each time you contact the library, please identify yourself as a Clarion University virtual campus student. You also will be asked to provide contact information so that resources may be e-mailed, faxed, or mailed to you.

The Library staff is committed to responding to your requests in a timely manner. While online technology makes many resources available in a very short time, library staff sometimes need to send materials by mail or contact other libraries for materials not owned by Clarion University. To insure that materials reach you in time to support your projects, please request resources as soon as possible.

Top of Page

Library Home Page
The Library Home Page serves as a gateway to journal and reference databases, online reference services, internet search engines, online interlibrary loan request forms, and links to other libraries. With few exceptions, resources are accessible from any location with Internet access.

 Top of Page

Library Catalog
The Library Catalog (PILOT) is the best way to check for availability of books, journals, and other materials in the University Libraries' collection. In addition to listing materials in the collection, the catalog provides links to many Internet resources and provides detailed holdings information for journal titles. PILOT also includes a simultaneous search feature, which enables a quick search of catalogs of other university libraries in the Keystone Library Network.

InterLibrary Loan Requests

The Interlibrary Loan staff makes every effort to help Virtual Campus students and faculty connect with needed resources. InterLibrary Loan (ILL) options are available on the Library Services page.  Virtual Campus faculty and students may use ILLiad to request materials owned by Clarion, as well as items which must be requested from other libraries.

Top of Page

Journal Articles
Before requesting an article via ILL, please check to see if the article you need is included in one of the full-text databases accessible through the Journals & Magazines A-Z List. Use the Journals & Magazines A-Z List link which is available on the Library Research Resources page to check availability. Type the journal name in the search box. If the title is available, a link or links to the database(s) will appear on the screen together with the time period covered. Follow the database links.

If the title is not available online, please use ILLiad under Library Services to order copies of needed articles.

  • You will be prompted for a login to access ILLiad, the software used by Clarion University to manage interlibrary loan requests. Click here for Step-by-step directions for using ILLiad. Click here to watch a video about using ILLiad.

  • Log into ILLiad with your Clarion username (without the "@clarion.edu") and your Clarion password.  This is the username you use to log into the MyClarion Portal, D2L, and Campus Computer Labs.  Example:  s_fmlast@clarion.edu    The first time you access ILLiad, you will also need to know your Library ID number.

    • Clarion University students: Your Library ID number is 9 + your Clarion ID number.

      • For example, if your Clarion ID is "12345678", your Library ID Login will be "912345678".

        • If you don't know your Clarion ID, you will be able to find it by following the instructions here.

    • Clarion University employees: Your Library ID Login is a nine-digit number consisting of a series of leading zeroes followed by your employee ID number.  (Use enough preceding zeroes to create a nine-digit number.)

      • For example, if your employee number is 98765, your Library ID Login is 000098765.

        • Your employee ID number can be found on your current pay stub.

Interlibrary loan staff will locate a library that has the article and then get it to you as quickly as possible. Frequently, the article is available in electronic format and can be made available to you within 3-7 days. ILLiad is used whenever possible. Otherwise articles are sent via U.S. postal service. If the source requested is difficult to locate or is available only in paper format, the time frame may be 7-14 days. Copies of articles do not need to be returned to the library.

Top of Page

Journal Articles - Tips

  • Make sure ILLiad request forms are completed as accurately and completely as possible. (If staff needs to verify the bibliographic information - or even your address - the process will take longer.)

  • When you register in ILLIad, provide your CU e-mail address. (If we need to e-mail information you, the University system is better equipped to handle large messages - like articles - than many commercial accounts.)

  • Be sure to keep your campus e-mail account cleared to ensure that articles sent to you are not rejected because of limited file space. Most of the time, however, articles will be delivered to you through the ILLiad software.

  • Request needed materials as early as possible. Clarion's interlibrary loan staff usually processes requests on the day received but turnaround time for filled requests varies considerably. Time frame depends on how readily available materials are elsewhere and the overall request volume at the lending library.

  • Book materials will be sent to the address you specify.

Renewals  
Books from the Clarion University collections may be renewed through PILOT, the Libraries' online catalog. After accessing the catalog, click on the "Renew Books" link at the top of the screen.

Click here to watch a video about how to renew books through PILOT.

Renewals of books from other libraries are permitted at the discretion of the lending library. If you need to renew an interlibrary loan item, please use the renewal request options in ILLiad or contact Clarion's Interlibrary Loan Department well before the due date.

Top of Page

Materials Not Available Through InterLibrary Loan

Occasionally the library will be unable to fill your request. Each library determines its own lending policies. For example: reference books, media, and dissertations are usually unavailable for loan. Other restrictions may also apply. If the InterLibrary Loan Department cannot fill your request, you will be notified as soon as possible through ILLiad.

Top of Page

Loan Periods
Books from the Clarion University Libraries' general collection circulate for four (4) weeks. Juvenile collection and media materials circulate for two (2) weeks. You may renew an item for a second loan period, if no hold has been placed on it. Distance education students may contact the ILL department at 814-393-2481 prior to the due date or by using the renew option available from the online catalog. (Click on the "Renew Books" link.)

Click here to watch a video about how to renew books through PILOT.

Fines are 20 cents per day for general collection items and $1 per day for periodical and reserve items. Please make checks payable to: Clarion University of Pennsylvania. To avoid fines, return or renew all materials by the due date.

Library materials that are 49 days overdue are considered "lost". Patrons are charged the replacement cost of "lost" books plus a $20 (per piece) lost item replacement fee.

Warning: Lost books may result in patron borrowing privileges being revoked until the items are returned and/or the account is cleared.

 On-Site Borrowing At Cooperating Libraries

  • PASSHE LIBRARIES

    • The PASSHE libraries will lend books directly to visiting faculty and students.

    • Your University ID is needed to borrow materials from these institutions.

    • Click here for a list of the PASSHE universities, and a map showing their locations.

  • LYRASIS

    • Students and faculty may use on-site resources at other libraries through our membership with LYRASIS.  Students and faculty must present valid photo ID and the LYRASIS Reciprocal Usage Form to borrow materials from participating libraries.  This form must be signed by a librarian from Clarion University before materials can be borrowed.  Therefore, you should obtain a signed form well in advance of your visit to a participating library.  Visit the reference desk to obtain a signed form, or contact Karen Sheesman in the reference department at 814-393-1841 to have a signed form mailed to you.    

  • ON-SITE ACCESS TO PHILADELPHIA AREA ACADEMIC LIBRARIES

    • UNIVERSITY OF PENNSYLVANIA LIBRARIES

      • Click here for an overview of the University of Pennsylvania visitor library use policy.

    • TEMPLE UNIVERSITY LIBRARIES:

      • It is strongly advised that you contact the Temple University Libraries with any specific questions that you have about access in advance of your visit. Click here for contact information.

        • To visit the library, bring your Clarion ID card and a valid driver's license. You will need to sign-in at the security guard's desk upon entering. You will be able to use the print materials on-site. However, you should visit the Reference Desk to inquire about the possibility of using the library's computers and databases on-site.
