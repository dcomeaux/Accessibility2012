[In 1900, 'Miss Waring' won a race associated with Charleston's Blockade Week festival. - 27398 Bytes] Visual Materials Division documents the people, places, and culture of South Carolina through a collection of 19th- and 20th-century photographs, postcards, illustrated newspapers, engravings, and lithographs. Other holdings include original artwork, sculpture, posters, blueprints, and architectural drawings.

Digitization and reproduction services are available. Please contact the visual materials archivist to arrange an appointment or discuss costs and delivery schedule.

Call 803-777-7090 or email Ms. Beth Bilderback

Addtional S.C. images available from:

  • South Carolina Political Collections.(photographs, political cartoons, etc.)
  • University Archives (USC people, buildings, campus, etc.)

[The Honorable James F. Byrnes (1882-1972) stands on a street in Conway, S.C., while on the campaign trail in 1939, posing with two full strings of fish borrowed from some passing children for the photo-op. - 13697 Bytes] To browse descriptions of many items held by Visual Materials Division, go to the online catalog, click on the pull-down menu and limit search to Columbia South Caroliniana Library.

To search by format, select Other Call Numberand type in:

  • Photo
  • Postcards
  • Artwork

When searching in KEYWORD mode, include the term PICTORIAL

The term will appear in the catalog along with the name of the person, location, event, school, organization, or business, as seen in the following examples of subject headings:

  • Strickland, Lily, 1887-1958 -- Pictorial works.
  • Ophir Gold Mines -- Pictorial works.
  • Columbia (S.C.) -- Pictorial works.
  • Stanley's China Hall (Columbia, S.C.) -- Pictorial works.
  • World War, 1939-1945 -- Germany -- Pictorial works.
