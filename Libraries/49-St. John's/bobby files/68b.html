Journal Rankings & Acceptance Rates

Academic journals are one of the main ways that scholars disseminate the results of their research and create new knowledge. Writing in Library Journal, Carol Tenopir estimated there were about 43,500 scholarly journals at the end of 2003. About 21,000 of these were refereed. Between 1/3 to 1/2 were online-only.

There are various methods to evaluate the relative rankings of academic journals. One way is to look at the acceptance rate versus the rejection rate to get a picture of how competitive it is. A journal's influence may also  be gauged by examining how often articles in its pages are cited in subsequent research. The links on this page use these and other methods to create journal rankings. They are meant to be used as a guide only.

Cabell's Directory of Publishing Opportunities provides acceptance rates, review process, and more information on over 3000 journals in the areas of Accounting, Economics & Finance, Management, Marketing, Education, Library Science, and Psychology.

Multiple Disciplines
Vienna University rates over 1800 international journals. The 2001 version covers Business Administration; Information Science; Economics; Mathematics and Statistics; Economic Geography; Regional and Environmental Sciences; Humanities; Language Science; Social Science; and Law.

SCI-Bites from ISI uses citation analysis to develop rankings of top 10 journals, hot papers, research fronts, and more.
