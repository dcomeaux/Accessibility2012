Vincentian Archives

The University Archives formerly housed records of the Eastern Province USA of the Congregation of the Mission, an evangelizing order founded in Paris by St. Vincent de Paul in the early 1600s. The Vincentians founded the Church of St. John the Baptist, St. John’s College, St. John’s Preparatory School, and St. John’s Seminary, all at the Lewis Avenue (Brooklyn) campus, between 1868 and 1891. The buildings still stand on the block bounded by Lewis Avenue, Hart, Willoughby, and Stuyvesant Streets. The Vincentian records moved in June, 1996, to new quarters at St. Vincent's Seminary, Philadelphia.

Fr. John Carven, C.M., is the archivist.
