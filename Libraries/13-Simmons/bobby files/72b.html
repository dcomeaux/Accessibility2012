Technology in the Library

Research & Resources

Technology in the Library

The Technology Desk on the first floor of the Library is the perfect place to go when you have a question about printing, copying, scanning, or checking out equipment.  You can reach them by phone at 617-521-2222 or online at the Service Desk.  For answers to quick questions, you can also use their FAQ page.

Using Multi-Function Devices in the Library

Guests and alumnae/i can obtain guest cards to use the multi-function devices from the Technology Desk on the first floor of the Library.

Printing

Print from any Simmons computer to any Campus Print printer.  Simply send the document to the print system, then release the document at any printer in the Library or on campus using your ID tap card.

With Campus Print, you can print from any non-Simmons computer to any printer, too.  Go to the Campus Print site, login, and upload your file to the system.  Then, release the document from any Campus Print printer in the Library or on campus using your ID tap card.  See Technology's for more information.

Copying

Copying is a breeze with the new ID tap card system.  Tap your card to initiate the machine, select Copy, and proceed as usual.  The multi-function devices copy single-sided, double-sided, on letter or legal size paper, in color (for some machines), and can even give you higher-quality copies for photos or Powerpoint slides.  See Technology's step-by-step explanation for more information.

Scan-to-Email

The multi-function devices also let you scan-to-email.  This great service eliminates paper waste and helps keep your important research all in one place — your computer!  Use your tap card to initiate the machine, then select Email.  Type in your email address or select it from a list of addresses.  Load your papers as you would to copy, then press Email It.  See Technology's step-by-step explanation for more information.

Technology Desk

Checking Out Equipment

Simmons student, faculty, staff, and alumnae/I may borrow laptops (PC or Apple) and other equipment (see list below) from the Technology Desk.  Laptops and other equipment are not allowed to leave the Library.  Only specified equipment may leave the Library.

Equipment Lending Policies
  • To check out items from the Technology Desk, users must read and sign the Laptop and Equipment Policy Agreement.
  • All laptops and equipment are due back within 3 hours, and can be renewed at the Technology Desk.
  • Fines of $20 per hour will be assessed for overdue items, and after 24 hours users will be charged for the entire cost of the equipment.

In-library loaned equipment include but is not limited to:

  • Headphones
  • Laptops
  • Mice
  • External floppy drives
  • Ethernet cables
  • VGA adapters
  • Mac dongles
  • Firewire cables

External library loan equipment include but is not limited to:

  • Digital still cameras
  • Digital video cameras
  • Analog SLR cameras
  • Voice recorders
  • External hard drives
