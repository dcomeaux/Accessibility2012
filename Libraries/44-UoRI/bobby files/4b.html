Circulation
      
Hours and telephone numbers

top

The Circulation Desk is open all hours that the Library is open. Books must be checked out 15 minutes prior to closing.

The Circulation Desk can be reached at (401) 874-2672.

For questions about circulation at the branch libraries, please call:

CCE:  Providence    (401) 277-5130
Pell:  Bay Campus    (401) 874-6161

Eligible borrowers

top

Anyone possessing a valid University ID card may have it validated to borrow books. Rhode Island residents 18 years of age or older, with proof of residency, may apply for a Special Borrower’s Card at the Circulation Desk.

The University Library is a member of the HELIN Library Consortium, which extends borrowing privileges to the faculty, students, and staff of the University of Rhode Island. Holdings of all of these libraries are included in the HELIN library catalog.

Special Borrowers do not have HELIN borrowing privileges. All persons who have outstanding library bills will be denied borrowing privileges. Borrowers must have their University ID or Special Borrower’s Card with them in order to check out library materials.

Responsibilities of borrowers

top

Borrowers are responsible for returning or renewing all items charged out on their cards. Borrowers are responsible for notifying the Library of any changes in contact information, including address, telephone number and e-mail.

Loan periods

top

Books, including some government publications, are loaned to borrowers for 28 days. Faculty and graduate students receive a 120-day loan. Serials (journals, magazines, and newspapers) and reference books do not circulate. Fines are imposed for overdue items (see Overdue books).

Charge-out procedures

top

The book(s) to be borrowed and a validated University ID card or a borrowers  card are given to the circulation attendant who charges the book out. The date due will be stamped on a slip in the back of the book.

Renewals

top

A borrowed book may be renewed if it has not been requested by another patron. Renewals should be made online or in person. Telephone the Circulation Desk at (401) 874-2672 if you have any questions. A book may be renewed three times before it must be returned. All renewals should be done prior to the actual due date.

Online E-Renewal:

  • Follow the instructions at http://www.uri.edu/library/circulation/erenewal.html.
  • To renew Reserves, Media, or Interlibrary Loan materials, please contact these departments directly.

To renew in person:

  • Bring the Library materials that you wish to renew to the Circulation Desk.
  • To renew Reserves, Media, or Interlibrary Loan materials, please go to these departments directly.

Interlibrary Loan renewals cannot be made at the Circulation Desk. A request to extend ILL material must go through Emily Greene in the ILL office at 401-874-4613 (Monday through Friday, 8:30am to 4:30pm).

HELIN loan

top

Books held by other HELIN libraries may be requested with the Request feature of the HELIN library catalog. Requestors will be prompted to enter their name, library barcode number, and a pickup location. Please note: If there are any letters at the beginning or end of your barcode, do not enter them as part of your barcode.

If the URI Library is designated as the pickup location, books requested through HELIN Loan will arrive at the Circulation desk, usually within one week of the request, and will be held for pickup for ten days. Borrowers will be notified via e-mail that the requested materials have arrived.

Holders of Special Borrowers cards may not request books through HELIN Loan.

Recalls

top

Books that are checked out may be requested by another patron by following the instructions for HELIN Loan above. When a book has been recalled, it must be returned to a staff member at the Circulation Desk by the due date on the recall notice. A fine of $3.00 per day is charged for overdue recalled books.

Search requests

top

Searches for books not found in the stacks or on the temporary book shelves may be requested by completing a book request card at the Circulation Desk.  When the book becomes available, it will be held at the Circulation Desk for ten days and the person requesting it will be notified.

Returning books

top

Books may be returned either to the Circulation Desk or placed in the book drop located to the west of the front door of the Library. Books borrowed from one HELIN library may be returned at any other HELIN library.

Overdue books

top

The fine for an overdue book is $0.50 per day. Recalled books are fined at $1.00 per day. Cash, checks, money orders, or RAM Account are accepted for payment of fines. When paying with cash, change may not be available for bills over $10.00. URI Faculty are not charged fines but are responsible for replacing lost items.

Lost books

top

A book six weeks overdue will be considered lost. Bills for lost books are calculated on the basis of the average price of a book ($80.00) plus a processing charge ($20.00). Borrowing privileges are automatically suspended and sanctions will be applied by the Registrar until the bill is settled. Refunds for books found and returned at a later date are made with the processing charge deducted.

Other services

top

The Circulation Unit in the University Library, Kingston coordinates a number of additional library services, such as administering group study rooms and lockers, providing a lost-and-found service, handling building emergencies, and opening and closing the building.

Closing procedures

top

A closing announcement is made 30, 15, and 5 minutes before closing. The building’s lights are flashed at these intervals. The Circulation Desk is closed for checkout 15 minutes prior to closing.

Contact information
