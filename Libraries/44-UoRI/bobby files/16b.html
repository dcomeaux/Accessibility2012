What’s New:
Search Savvy Seminars, 2012 - Tips & Tricks to Improve Your Information Retrieval Skills

Friday afternoons 1 – 3 PM in Library LL12

This semester the URI Libraries will again be sponsoring a series of training sessions to help improve your research skills. The sessions will be given once a month by Associate Professor Amanda Izenstark. The seminars will be on Friday afternoons from 1-3 PM in room LL12 in the Robert L. Carothers Library & Learning Commons, Kingston. All are welcome to attend.

February 10 - RefWorks
Manage citations and references with ease using RefWorks. Whether you want to learn about the features of the new version, get a refresher on RefWorks' functionality, or start using RefWorks from scratch, this session will help you manage citations, share reference lists with collaborators or classes, and use other features of RefWorks 2.0.

March 2 - Library & Online Research Tips & Tricks
From advanced Boolean searching in databases and search engines to using plug-ins to automate catalog searches and link to articles, this session offers a variety of time-saving tips and tricks to make your online and library research faster and easier. (Because some tools require installation on one's own computer, attendees are encouraged to bring their own laptop, but it is not required.)

April 13 - Google Like a Librarian
Google isn't just a single search box - it offers much more under that top layer with the cute logos. This session will cover tricks to target your searches precisely as well as techniques to harness the power of Google Scholar, Google Books, and Google Docs for your own work or for classes.

For further information on the University Libraries' Search Savvy Seminars series, please contact Robin Devin, Head of Reference, at rdevin@uri.edu or (401) 874-2128.
