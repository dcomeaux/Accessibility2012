Borrowing from other libraries

You cannot request an item from another library through Interlibrary Loan if we own a copy.

Check the Catalogue to be sure, or Ask us.

Check the McGill WorldCat Catalogue, COLOMBO, or Other library catalogues to locate items held by other libraries.

There are three ways to borrow material not available at the McGill Library.

1. Borrow directly from other universities

Students and faculty can acquire a CREPUQ card which entitles them to borrow directly from other Canadian university libraries.

2. Interlibrary Loan (ILL)

Use Interlibrary Loan (ILL) to obtain journal articles and books not in the collection of the McGill Library.

Music recordings

Music recordings are rarely circulated through inter-library loan. If there is a music recording (audio or video) you need but were not able to locate either in the Library catalogue or in the streaming audio/video collection, you can submit a suggestion to the Marvin Duchow Music Library for purchase consideration.

Life Sciences ILL

Life Sciences ILL
  • Request a book
  • Request an article

Life Sciences Library Interlibrary Loan should be used by students and faculty in

  • the Faculty of Medicine (and its affiliated schools and institutes)
  • the Faculty of Dentistry (and its affiliated schools and institutes)
  • the Department of Biology
  • and for those members of the Redpath Museum or the Department of Psychology whose research and study information is primarily found in the Life Sciences Library.

Who is eligible for Interlibrary Loan services?

Interlibrary Loan service is available to students, faculty and staff who hold currently-valid McGill photo-ID cards, as well as to retired faculty. Continuing Education students currently registered in a certificate or diploma program may request material related to courses in the program. Library accounts must be in good standing, e.g. not blocked due to unpaid fines.

What can be requested on Interlibrary Loan?

Interlibrary Loan service supports the academic and research needs of faculty and students; non-academic staff may request work-related materials.

What cannot be requested on Interlibrary Loan?

Due to financial limitations, the ILL service cannot accommodate requests related to extracurricular reading, hobbies and recreational interests, or requests submitted on behalf of non-McGill clientele.

How much does ILL cost?

The ILL service is free. McGill University Library subsidizes the cost of all regular ILL requests. Charges, however, may be applied for urgent delivery.

How long does ILL take?

Response time varies from 2 days to 3 weeks depending on the requested material and the supplier.
