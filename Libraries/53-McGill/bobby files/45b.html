Computers & equipment

Computers are available in all McGill branch libraries and run on the Windows XP operating system.

All McGill branch libraries are part of the McGill Wireless Zone, allowing McGill students and staff who own laptops to connect to the Internet.

  • Software in the Library
  • Equipment in the Library
  • Computer workstations in the Library

Borrow a laptop

[Borrow a laptop from the Library] Laptops can be borrowed from:

  • Education
  • Howard Ross
  • Humanities and Social Sciences
  • Macdonald Campus
  • Life Sciences
  • Marvin Duchow Music
  • Nahum Gelber Law
  • Schulich Science and Engineering
  • Laptops can be borrowed by any authorized McGill users, including students and faculty.
  • Borrowing period: up to 48 hours. More information on borrowing laptops.
  • Software: same as public computers found in the Library.
  • Connecting to the wireless network: instructions are provided on the laptop screen.
  • Laptops are equipped with software that erases everything that the user has saved or done the moment they log off, except for files the user chooses to save on the D: drive.
  • Most of the laptops available for loan are Windows-based laptops. A smaller number of Mac laptops are available at each branch that loans laptops.

Users can save files

  • On USB drives or CDs
  • Their P: drive. Instructions on how to connect to the P: drive are available on the desktop.

Information on borrowing period, renewals, fines

See our borrowers' guides for students and staff for more information. You will be required to wait one hour before borrowing the same laptop to ensure equitable access for all.

Some library branches have been lending laptops for some time prior to this initiative, often with specialized software installed for students within a specific discipline. At this time, there is no change to current borrowing practices for those laptops.

Authentication and access

McGill students and staff

  • McGill Username and McGill Password are used to log in to Library computers
  • This gives access to software, hardware, and electronic resources
  • Save files on your personal drive (P: drive for students or R: drive for staff)

Visiting scholars

A Guest Network Account may be set up to support, on a short-term immediate basis, academic pursuits by visiting scholars which are consistent with the scholarly teaching, learning and research activities of McGill University. The Guest Network Account is not intended to provide library visitors with frequent or ongoing access to library computer resources, nor casual access to the internet, nor off-campus access.

Please ask library staff about Guest Network Accounts.

Guest access

Clients accessing computer workstations in the Library by logging in as a guest (Username: guest [dot] library [at] mcgill [dot] ca; Password: guest123) are welcome to access most of our electronic resources. Please note that guest accounts do not provide access to unlimited web browsing, software applications, and hardware other than what is required to view, print, and download documents. Some licensed resources are also restricted to members of the McGill community. For more information about printing see copying and printing.

Use of library computers

  • McGill students, faculty and staff have priority for the use of library computers.
  • Computers are limited in number and cannot be reserved at any time.
  • Computers left unattended for longer than 5 minutes will be made available to the next user.
  • Computers logout automatically after 15 minutes of inactivity.
  • Unattended personal belongings will be removed by staff.
